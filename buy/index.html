Buyer Guide

Buy a home in Seattle

A clear roadmap from first tour to keys in hand, with calm, data-backed decisions at every step.

1

Discovery call & needs analysis

Align on budget, timing, neighborhoods, and must-have features so the search starts focused.

20–30 min

We frame the search so every tour and comp review has context.

  • Must-haves vs nice-to-haves, renovation comfort, parking, natural light.
  • Lifestyle fit: commute, schools, walkability, noise, and micro-location.
  • Set up a custom search + price-cut alerts that reflect real-time market shifts.
2

Local lender pre-approval & budget

Strengthen your offer and confirm a monthly payment range before touring.

1–3 days
  • Compare rates + fees with trusted Seattle lenders; request desktop underwrite when possible.
  • Understand taxes/HOA/insurance in the monthly number; watch debt-to-income.
  • Have funds ready for earnest money; proofs prepared for offer week.
3

Neighborhood preview + tour plan

Compare areas, tour actives, and refine alerts based on lifestyle and value trends.

ongoing
  • Walk/drive at different times; note street activity, noise, and parking.
  • Review comps and price history; track days-on-market and price changes with the weekly reports I send.
  • Focus on homes with strong fundamentals: layout, light, lot, structure.
4

Offer strategy: price, terms, timing

Structure a competitive offer based on data and seller priorities.

same day
  • Price from recent comps + absorption; consider escalation or appraisal gap only when justified.
  • Contingencies: inspection, financing, HOA (condos); dial protection vs speed intentionally.
  • Clean presentation: earnest money, proofs, timelines, and clear communication.
5

Inspection + HOA review (condos)

Focused due diligence on the home and building to manage long-term risk.

3–7 days
  • General inspection; add sewer scope, roof, structural, or environmental as needed.
  • Condos: resale certificate, reserves, budget health, rules, special assessments, and minutes.
  • Negotiate repairs/credits or exit per contract if material issues arise.
6

Closing prep: appraisal → keys

Track appraisal, underwriting, signing, utilities, and the final walk-through.

2–4 weeks
  • Coordinate appraisal; plan for value gaps only if comps support it.
  • Final loan docs, escrow/title, insurance, and wire safety.
  • Final walk-through checklist; keys and move-in logistics.
